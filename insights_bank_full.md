## 1. Campaign Funnel Overview

**Finding:**
Out of 44,920 contacts made, only 5,255 subscribed to the term
deposit — an 11.7% conversion rate. Nearly 88% of all contacts
do not convert, suggesting that either the targeting is broad
and unfocused, or the campaign messaging is not resonating
with the majority of the audience.

**Recommendation:**
Use the insights from this analysis to build a targeted contact
list. Prioritising high-probability segments (students, retired,
cellular contacts, previous successful subscribers) would
dramatically improve the conversion rate without increasing
call volume.

---

## 2. Contact Channel Performance

**Finding:**
Cellular phone contacts convert at a significantly higher rate
than telephone (landline) contacts. Contacts where the channel
was previously unknown (now filled as cellular based on mode)
sit at the average. This confirms that customers are more
receptive and accessible on mobile phones than landlines.

**Recommendation:**
Build and maintain a cellular contact database as a priority.
For contacts where only a landline number is available, consider
supplementing with email or SMS outreach before calling to warm
up the lead.

---

## 3. Best Months to Call

**Finding:**
March, September, October and December show the highest
conversion rates — well above the 11.7% average. May is the
highest volume month (over 13,000 calls) but converts at
well below average, meaning the majority of call centre
effort is being spent in the least effective month.

**Recommendation:**
Dramatically reduce outreach in May and redirect resources
to March and the Q4 months (September-December). Running
focused campaigns in these months with a smaller, better
targeted contact list will produce more subscriptions at
lower cost.

---

## 4. Call Duration vs Conversion

**Finding:**
Call duration is the strongest individual predictor of
conversion. Calls under 1 minute convert at under 1%,
while calls over 5 minutes convert at over 40%. This makes
logical sense — a customer who stays on the call for over
5 minutes is genuinely interested in the product.

**Recommendation:**
Train agents to extend quality conversations rather than
rushing through scripts. Introduce quality monitoring to
flag calls that drop below 2 minutes — these represent
wasted effort where the agent is not engaging the customer.

---

## 5. Number of Calls vs Conversion

**Finding:**
First calls convert at the highest rate. After 3 calls to
the same contact without conversion, the rate drops
significantly and continues declining. Contacts who have
received 6 or more calls convert at near-zero rates,
representing a major waste of agent time.

**Recommendation:**
Implement a hard limit of 3 call attempts per contact per
campaign. After 3 unsuccessful attempts, move the contact
to a lower-cost channel (email, SMS) or a future campaign
rather than continuing expensive outbound calls.

---

## 6. Best Job Segments

**Finding:**
Students and retired individuals convert at the highest
rates — significantly above the 11.7% average. Management
and admin roles also perform above average. Blue-collar
and services workers convert at the lowest rates, suggesting
these segments have less interest in or need for term deposits.

**Recommendation:**
Build separate targeting strategies for high-converting
segments. For student and retired contacts, use tailored
scripts that speak to their specific financial situations
(savings goals, fixed income planning). Reduce call volume
to blue-collar and services segments.

---

## 7. Previous Campaign Impact

**Finding:**
Customers who successfully subscribed in a previous campaign
convert at an extremely high rate (over 60%) in the current
campaign. Customers who were previously contacted but did
not subscribe (failure outcome) convert at below-average
rates. Customers with no previous contact sit at the average.

**Recommendation:**
Create a dedicated "previous success" re-engagement list
for every new campaign. These contacts should always be
called first as they represent the highest ROI with the
least effort. Consider giving these contacts a loyalty
incentive such as a preferential interest rate.

---

## 8. Age Group Analysis

**Finding:**
The 18-25 (student) and 65+ (retired) age groups convert
at the highest rates. Middle-aged groups (36-55) convert
at or below average, likely because they have more
financial commitments (mortgages, children) that reduce
their appetite for locking money in a term deposit.

**Recommendation:**
Target young adults (18-25) with messaging around savings
habits and financial independence. Target 65+ customers
with messaging around capital preservation and fixed
returns. Both groups respond well to security-focused
financial products.

---

## Summary of Key Recommendations

1. Prioritise cellular contacts — converts 2x better than telephone
2. Target students and retired customers — highest converting segments
3. Focus campaigns on March, September, October and December
4. Train agents to keep calls above 3 minutes for best results
5. Stop calling after 3 attempts — ROI drops sharply after this
6. Always re-contact previous successful subscribers first
7. Reduce call volume to blue-collar and services segments
